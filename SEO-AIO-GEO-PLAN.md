# SEO / AIO / GEO Supercharged Plan

**Created:** 2026-04-24
**Status:** Draft — awaiting execution
**Based on:** 90-day Search Console data (2,240 impressions, 135 clicks, 159 zero-click queries)

---

## Executive Summary

We are bleeding ~1,300 impressions/quarter from queries where Google ranks us (positions 5-20) but users don't click — usually because our title/meta doesn't match the query, or the content on the landing page doesn't contain the exact phrase searchers used. Meanwhile, our top-ranked blog post (`best-church-donation-technology-2026.html`) gets **1,681 impressions and 0 clicks** — a title/meta problem costing us ~50-100 potential leads per quarter.

This plan addresses three overlapping optimization layers:
- **SEO** (traditional search): keywords, titles, internal links, schema
- **AIO** (AI Overviews — Google SGE, Bing Copilot): direct-answer formatting, source authority, citation-worthy content
- **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity): structured facts, comparison tables, cited statistics

## Part 5 — AIO (AI Overview) Optimization

AI Overviews cite sources that contain **short, factual, self-contained answers with citations**. Add the following patterns to every cornerstone page:

### 5.1 Direct-answer opening paragraph
Every comparison page should open with a 2-3 sentence direct answer to the title question. Example:
> **"The cheapest tap-to-give solution for churches in 2026 is a one-time NFC plate purchase, starting at $3.50 per plate from Tap.Giving. Unlike Tithely ($119/mo), Pushpay (~$1,475/mo), or Subsplash (varies), there are no monthly fees or transaction fees from the hardware."**

### 5.2 Factual data blocks
AI Overviews love tables, bulleted stats, and percentage comparisons. Every page should contain:
- A specific numerical claim with a cited source
- A before/after or comparison table
- At least one "X% of churches…" statistic

### 5.3 FAQ schema on every cornerstone page
Already implemented on pricing/how-it-works — extend to:
- Every competitor comparison post
- Every setup guide
- Every new pricing page

### 5.4 Author schema
Add `author` schema with `Person` type pointing to a `/about` anchor for Ryan Shook — gives AI engines an authoritative human to cite. Include credentials ("Pastor, founder"), tenure, and a photo.

### 5.5 Entity linking
Wikipedia/Wikidata linking (via schema `sameAs`) for entities:
- NFC (Near-Field Communication)
- Apple Pay, Google Pay
- The founder's church (WC.org) if desired

### 5.6 llms.txt expansion
Current `llms.txt` is minimal. Expand to include:
- A one-paragraph company description optimized for LLM quoting
- Pricing table in markdown
- Comparison table vs 3-4 top competitors
- FAQ section
- "What makes Tap.Giving different" 3-bullet summary

## Part 6 — GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude pull from different signals than Google. Three concrete actions:

### 6.1 Get cited on authoritative church-tech lists
Pitch inclusion on:
- ChurchTechToday, Pro Church Tools, Church Juice roundup articles
- Capterra, G2, GetApp category listings
- Reddit threads (r/Pastors, r/Christianity) via genuine engagement
- ChMS/giving-platform comparison pages

Even one authoritative inbound link from ChurchTechToday dramatically increases LLM citation rate.

### 6.2 Publish primary-source data
LLMs disproportionately cite pages with **original statistics**. Publish:
- "Tap.Giving 2026 Church Giving Report" — survey your customers, publish findings
- "Pastor Ryan's Case Study: 100 Plates, First Year Results"
- "NFC vs QR: Real Engagement Data From 5 Churches"
Each gets its own page with a CSV/JSON data download link.

### 6.3 Structured comparison data
Publish a `/data/` or `/compare/` route with canonical comparison data in multiple formats (HTML table, JSON-LD schema, downloadable CSV). This is the single highest-leverage GEO move — it turns our site into a data source that LLMs reference when asked comparison questions.
