Our Story

A Simple Idea Born in the Pews

Tap.Giving was founded in 2024 by Ryan Shook, an active pastor at a large church in the Houston, Texas area. After his own congregation adopted NFC tap-to-give plates, he launched Tap.Giving to make the same hardware available to other churches at cost — $3.50–$4.50 per plate, one-time, no monthly fees. Tap.Giving plates have shipped to churches across the United States, including Baptist, Methodist, non-denominational, and Catholic congregations, all working with existing giving platforms like Tithely, Pushpay, Subsplash, Donorbox, and Planning Center.

I’m a pastor. And like most pastors, I’ve watched my church’s budget get squeezed from every direction — utilities, insurance, maintenance, staff. So when it came time to explore digital giving, the last thing I wanted was another monthly subscription.

But that’s exactly what I found. Platform after platform charging $50–$200 a month — just to collect donations that were already being processed through Tithely, Subsplash, or Planning Center. I watched other churches agonize over the same decision: “Do we really add one more subscription to an already tight budget?”

Then it hit me. NFC technology is remarkably simple. A chip stores a URL. A phone reads it. That’s it. There’s no reason this needs a $100/month platform. No reason it needs a login, a dashboard, or a sales call. It just needs to work.

So I built Tap.Giving. The concept is straightforward: buy the plates once, point them at your existing giving page, and never pay another fee. Your congregation taps their phone during the offering, their giving page opens, and they give — right there in the moment, together.

The mission isn’t to replace your giving platform. It’s to give your congregation a physical, communal way to access it. Because the offering moment matters — and it shouldn’t cost your church $100 a month to keep it alive.

What We Believe

The Values Behind Tap.Giving

Every decision we make comes back to these principles.

Churches Shouldn’t Overpay for Simplicity

NFC is a $0.50 chip. The technology that makes tap-to-give possible is inexpensive and widely available. There’s no reason it should cost your church $100 a month. We believe the price should reflect the simplicity.

The Offering Moment Matters

Digital giving is great, but it shouldn’t kill the communal act of passing the plate. There’s something powerful about a congregation giving together in the same moment. Tap.Giving keeps both — the convenience of digital and the togetherness of the offering.

No Lock-In, Ever

Your plates work with any giving platform — Tithely, Subsplash, Planning Center, Pushpay, Givelify, or any other. Switch providers whenever you want. The plates stay the same. You’re never stuck.

Every Church Deserves This

From a 30-person church plant to a 5,000-seat megachurch, the price per plate is the same. No enterprise tiers. No “call us for a quote.” No sales calls required. Just simple, fair pricing for every church.
