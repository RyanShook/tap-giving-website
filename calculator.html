              How is the ROI for NFC giving plates calculated?
            

            

              The calculation uses your church's average attendance, visitor count, and average gift amount. It estimates
              new weekly gifts from visitors using industry data showing
              53% of NFC givers are first-time
                givers. It then projects annual giving increase, compares it against the one-time plate cost, and
              calculates your break-even timeline and first-year ROI percentage.
            

          


          

            

              What assumptions does the calculator use?
            

            

              We use conservative, research-backed assumptions: a 53% first-time giver conversion rate for visitors who
              interact with NFC plates, your entered average gift amount, and actual
              Tap.Giving volume
                pricing ($3.50–$4.50 per plate). Competitor platform costs are based on publicly available
              pricing.
            

          


          

            

              How much do churches save with NFC plates vs. giving
              platform subscriptions?
            

            

              Savings vary by platform. Churches using Pushpay ($17,700/year) can save over $85,000 over 5 years.
              Even compared to more affordable platforms like Tithely Plus ($1,428/year), churches save thousands because
              Tap.Giving plates are a one-time purchase with no recurring fees. Check out our
              complete church giving technology
                guide for a full breakdown.
            

          


          

            

              Can I share the ROI results with my finance committee?
            

            

              Yes. The calculator includes a ready-to-share summary section above that formats your custom results into a
              clear, copy-paste-friendly format designed for church finance committees and leadership teams. Just click
              "Copy to Clipboard" and paste it into an email.
