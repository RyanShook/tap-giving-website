# Tap.Giving Brand Voice & Messaging

This document defines the Tap.Giving brand voice, tone, and messaging framework. Reference this when writing all content to ensure consistency.

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Pastor-Hearted
- **What it means**: We understand ministry. Our founder is a pastor. We know the pressures of church budgets, volunteer capacity, and the desire to make giving frictionless for congregants.
- **How it sounds**: Empathetic, relatable, mission-aligned. We speak as insiders who genuinely care about the church's success — not as salespeople.
- **Example**: "We built Tap.Giving because we saw the same thing you see every Sunday — people who want to give but don't carry cash, fumble with apps, or just forget."
- **Avoid**: Cold corporate tone, jargon-heavy tech speak, talking down to church leaders, treating churches as "customers" rather than partners in ministry.

### 2. Straightforward & Honest
- **What it means**: We lead with transparency. Our pricing is simple. Our product is simple. We don't hide fees or oversell capabilities.
- **How it sounds**: Direct, clear, no-nonsense. Short sentences. Real numbers. We say what we do and what we don't do.
- **Example**: "We don't process payments. We don't charge monthly fees. We sell NFC plates that open your church's existing giving page when someone taps their phone. That's it."
- **Avoid**: Hype, buzzwords ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive"), vague promises, hiding limitations, inflated claims.

### 3. Data-Backed
- **What it means**: We support our claims with real statistics and research. Churches are stewards of donated money — they need to justify purchases to boards and committees.
- **How it sounds**: Confident, credible, specific. We cite studies, reference industry data, and use real numbers.
- **Example**: "Churches using NFC tap-to-give see an average 300% increase in donations at the point of collection. NFC is 42x more effective than QR codes for engagement."
- **Avoid**: Unsourced claims, vague language ("studies show..."), making up statistics, over-promising ROI.

### 4. Affordable Champion
- **What it means**: We position ourselves as the most cost-effective option. We're on the side of churches that don't have enterprise budgets.
- **How it sounds**: Value-conscious, empowering. We help churches see that modern giving technology doesn't require a $20K/year subscription.
- **Example**: "For less than $5 per plate, your entire congregation gets tap-to-give access — with zero monthly fees, zero transaction fees from us, and zero setup costs."
- **Avoid**: Disparaging competitors by name in negative ways, implying cheap = low quality, apologizing for being affordable.

### 5. Practically Helpful
- **What it means**: Every piece of content should give the reader something they can use — a decision framework, a setup guide, a comparison chart, a troubleshooting tip.
- **How it sounds**: Action-oriented, organized, step-by-step. We respect the reader's time by being thorough but not bloated.
- **Example**: "Here's exactly how to set up NFC giving with Tithely in under 10 minutes — step by step, with screenshots."
- **Avoid**: Fluff content that talks around a topic, long introductions before getting to the point, content that exists only for SEO without providing real value.

## Tone Guidelines

### General Tone: Warm Professional
Imagine you're a fellow pastor or church operations director helping a colleague at a conference. You know your stuff, you genuinely want to help, and you explain things clearly without being condescending. You're the person who says "here, let me show you" instead of handing over a 50-page manual.

### Tone Variations by Content Type

**How-To / Setup Guides**: Clear, patient, step-by-step
- "First, log into your Tithely dashboard."
- "Don't worry if this looks complicated — it takes about 5 minutes."
- "Once you've saved your giving URL, you're ready to order plates."

**Competitor Comparisons**: Fair, factual, confident
- "Pushpay is an excellent platform. But if you're paying $20K/year and only need tap-to-give, there's a simpler option."
- "Here's what each solution costs over 5 years — you decide what makes sense for your church."
- "We're not trying to replace Tithely. We work alongside it."

**Educational / Thought Leadership**: Authoritative, research-driven, forward-looking
- "Digital giving now represents 41% of total church donations where it's offered."
- "The data is clear: reducing friction at the point of giving increases both participation and average gift size."
- "NFC technology has been in every smartphone since 2014 — the infrastructure is already in your congregation's pockets."

**Seasonal Strategy Content**: Urgent but not pushy, practical
- "Easter is your highest-attendance Sunday. Make sure visitors can give without downloading an app."
- "Christmas Eve is coming. Here's a 4-week rollout plan for tap-to-give."
- "Year-end giving accounts for 30% of annual church revenue. Don't leave it to chance."

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Sentence Structure
- **Vary length**: Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones. A short sentence after a long one creates emphasis.
- **Active voice preferred**: "Churches save $7,000 over 5 years" not "Over $7,000 is saved by churches over 5 years"
- **Average length**: 12–20 words per sentence
- **Clarity first**: If a sentence needs to be read twice to understand, rewrite it.

### Paragraph Structure
- **Length**: 2–4 sentences typically. Never more than 5.
- **One idea per paragraph**: Each paragraph should make one point.
- **Transitions**: Use natural connectors, not forced transition words.
- **White space**: Generous spacing. Church leaders are scanning on phones between meetings.

### Word Choice
- **Conversational**: Write how you'd explain it to a fellow church leader over coffee
- **Specific**: "$450 for 100 plates" not "affordable pricing"
- **Active verbs**: "Tap," "Give," "Save," "Mount," "Launch," "Set up"
- **Cut the fluff**: Remove "very," "really," "actually," "just," "simply" unless they add meaning

### Terminology

**Say This** → **Not That**
- tap-to-give → contactless giving (more specific to our product)
- NFC plates → NFC tags/stickers (plates sounds more premium and intentional)
- giving page/giving URL → donation link (matches church vocabulary)
- congregants/attenders → users/customers (church language)
- church → organization/entity (keep it personal)
- one-time cost → capex/capital expenditure (keep it simple)
- works with → integrates with (we're not a software integration)

## Content Formatting

### Headlines
- **Be specific**: "How to Set Up NFC Giving with Tithely in 10 Minutes" not "Getting Started with Digital Giving"
- **Include benefit**: Tell the reader what they'll gain or learn
- **Use numbers**: "Save $7,340 Over 5 Years" or "3 Steps to Launch Tap-to-Give"
- **Keep concise**: 60 characters or less for SEO, can be longer for H2s

### Subheadings
- **Scannable**: A reader skimming only H2s should understand the article's arc
- **Keyword-rich**: Include search terms naturally
- **Parallel structure**: Keep formatting consistent within an article
- **Question format welcome**: "How Much Does NFC Giving Cost?" works great for featured snippets

### Lists
- **Use for 3+ related items**: Don't put 2 items in a bullet list
- **Parallel structure**: Start each item the same way (all verbs, all nouns, etc.)
- **Fragment or sentence, not mixed**: Pick one style and stick with it per list
- **Bold lead-ins for definition lists**: **Feature**: Description format

### Calls-to-Action
- **Primary CTA**: "Get Your Free Quote" / "Order Your Plates" / "See Pricing"
- **Secondary CTA**: "Learn How It Works" / "Read the Setup Guide"
- **Tone**: Inviting, not pushy. "Ready to bring tap-to-give to your church?" not "BUY NOW"
- **Placement**: After establishing value, not before

## Voice Examples

### Excellent Tap.Giving Voice ✅

"Most churches don't need a $20,000-a-year giving platform just to let people tap their phones. Tap.Giving plates cost $3.50–$4.50 each, work with whatever giving platform you already use, and there are no monthly fees. A church with 200 seats can be fully equipped for $800 — less than one month of what some competitors charge. Just mount the plates, and your congregation can give by tapping their phone. No app download. No account creation. No friction."

**Why this works**:
- Leads with the pain point (overpaying)
- Gives specific pricing immediately
- Reinforces platform compatibility
- Uses concrete comparison ($800 vs one month of competitor fees)
- Ends with the simplicity message

### Not Tap.Giving Voice ❌

"Tap.Giving is a revolutionary NFC-powered digital giving solution that leverages cutting-edge near-field communication technology to transform the way your organization collects tithes and offerings. Our innovative platform seamlessly integrates with your existing technology stack to deliver a frictionless, next-generation giving experience for your stakeholders."

**Why this fails**:
- Buzzword-heavy ("revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "next-generation")
- Corporate jargon ("stakeholders," "technology stack," "leverages")
- Says nothing specific or useful
- Feels like enterprise software marketing, not church ministry
- No pricing, no proof points, no humanity

## Audience Understanding

### Who We Write For

**Primary Audience**: Church Decision Makers
- Pastors (lead, executive, or administrative)
- Church administrators and office managers
- Finance committee members or treasurers
- Elders/deacons involved in operations
- They're budget-conscious, time-constrained, and skeptical of tech vendors

**Secondary Audiences**:
- **Church Tech/AV Teams**: Want technical specs, compatibility info, troubleshooting
- **Denominational Leaders**: Looking for solutions to recommend across multiple churches
- **Church Planters**: Starting from scratch, need affordable infrastructure
- **Worship/Creative Directors**: Interested in the look and feel, how it fits the worship space

### What They Care About

**Top Priorities**:
1. Cost — "How much will this cost our church?"
2. Simplicity — "How hard is this to set up and maintain?"
3. Compatibility — "Does it work with what we already use?"
4. Effectiveness — "Will people actually use it?"
5. Trust — "Is this company legitimate? Will they be around next year?"

**Pain Points**:
1. Giving platforms are expensive and getting more expensive
2. Congregants don't carry cash but fumble with apps
3. Visitors have no idea how to give during a service
4. QR codes feel impersonal and have low scan rates
5. Leadership committees are skeptical of new technology investments

### How to Serve Them

- **Respect their budget**: Always lead with cost transparency. No bait-and-switch.
- **Make it actionable**: Every article should end with a clear next step they can take today.
- **Speak their language**: Use church vocabulary (tithes, offerings, congregants, ministry) not tech/startup jargon.
- **Address the committee**: Write content that helps a champion sell this internally ("share this comparison with your finance team").
- **Be honest about limitations**: We don't do payment processing. We don't have an app. Own it — it builds trust.
