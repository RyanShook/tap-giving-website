# Tap.Giving Features & Benefits

This document outlines Tap.Giving's key features, benefits, and differentiators to inform content creation that drives lead capture and order conversions.

## Core Value Propositions

### 1. **NFC Tap-to-Give Plates**
- **Feature**: Custom-printed 100mm (4") diameter NFC plates with NTAG215 chips, pre-encoded with the church's giving URL. Available with adhesive backing, pre-drilled holes for screws, or elastic bands for chairs.
- **Benefit**: Congregants tap their phone on the plate and go directly to the church's giving page — no app, no login, no typing a URL. Works with every modern smartphone.
- **Conversion Angle**: "Turn every seat into a giving station. One tap opens your church's giving page — no app required."

### 2. **One-Time Purchase, No Recurring Fees**
- **Feature**: Plates are purchased outright at $3.50–$4.50 per plate depending on quantity. No monthly subscription, no setup fee, no transaction fee from Tap.Giving.
- **Benefit**: Churches pay once and own their hardware forever. No ongoing budget line item, no surprise fee increases, no vendor lock-in.
- **Conversion Angle**: "Stop renting your giving technology. Own it outright — starting at $3.50 per plate."

### 3. **Platform Agnostic — Works With Any Giving Platform**
- **Feature**: Plates are encoded with any URL the church provides. Works with Tithely, Pushpay, Givelify, Donorbox, Anedot, Planning Center, Subsplash, Nucleus, or any giving platform with a web URL.
- **Benefit**: Churches don't need to switch payment processors or giving platforms. Tap.Giving adds a new entry point to their existing system.
- **Conversion Angle**: "Keep your giving platform. Add our plates. It's that simple."

### 4. **Custom Branding**
- **Feature**: Full custom printing on each plate. Church provides logo and branding; plates are printed in full color to match the church's identity.
- **Benefit**: Plates look professional and intentional — not like an afterthought. They become part of the worship environment.
- **Conversion Angle**: "Your logo, your colors, your church's identity — on every plate."

### 5. **No App Download Required**
- **Feature**: NFC triggers the phone's native browser to open the giving URL. No app installation, no account creation, no QR code scanning.
- **Benefit**: First-time visitors can give within seconds of tapping, with zero setup on their end. Eliminates the #1 barrier to digital giving adoption.
- **Conversion Angle**: "Your visitors don't need to download anything. They just tap and give."

### 6. **Free Shipping**

- **Benefit**: No hidden costs at checkout. The price you see is the price you pay.

## Technical Features

### NFC Hardware
- **NTAG215 chips**: Industry-standard NFC chip, compatible with all NFC-enabled smartphones
- **100mm (4") diameter**: Large enough to be visible and tappable, small enough to mount discreetly
- **3mm bleed printing**: Full-color, edge-to-edge custom printing
- **Adhesive backing**: Standard on all plates, strong enough for pew backs and walls
- **Pre-drilled holes**: Optional, for screw mounting on pews or chairs
- **Elastic bands**: Optional, for attaching to chair backs (~111.8cm for standard chairs)
- **URL encoding**: Pre-encoded before shipping, locked or unlocked per church preference
- **Password protection**: Available but not recommended (requires NFC desk writer to change URL later)

### Phone Compatibility
- **iPhone**: iPhone 7 and later (iOS 14+), NFC reads natively — no app needed
- **Android**: Most Android phones from 2015+ with NFC enabled
- **Samsung**: Galaxy S6 and later
- **Google Pixel**: All Pixel phones
- **No app required**: Phones open the URL directly in the default browser

### Mounting Options
- **Adhesive back**: Standard on all plates, industrial-strength adhesive
- **Screws**: Pre-drilled holes available, ZBTech can include screws
- **Elastic bands**: Black elastic bands for chair backs
- **Velcro**: Available for removable mounting
- **Nano double-sided tape**: Extra adhesive option for challenging surfaces

## Common Questions & Objections

### "Do we need to switch giving platforms?"
**Answer**: No. Tap.Giving works with whatever giving platform you already use — Tithely, Pushpay, Givelify, Donorbox, or any platform with a web giving URL. We're hardware, not software. Your plates just open your existing giving page.

### "What if someone's phone doesn't have NFC?"
**Answer**: Every iPhone since iPhone 7 (2016) and the vast majority of Android phones since 2015 have NFC. That covers well over 95% of smartphones in use today. For the rare phone without NFC, your giving page URL is also printed on the plate.

### "Is NFC giving secure?"
**Answer**: Yes. NFC plates simply open a URL — the same as typing a web address into a browser. All payment processing happens through your giving platform's secure, PCI-compliant checkout. No financial data touches the NFC chip.

### "How long do the plates last?"
**Answer**: NFC chips have no battery and no moving parts. They last essentially forever. The printed plate itself is durable and designed for long-term mounting.

### "Can we change the URL later?"
**Answer**: If your plates are shipped unlocked, yes — you can reprogram them with any NFC writing app. If locked, the URL is permanent. We recommend locking for security (prevents tampering) and providing your best long-term URL.

## Content Creation Guidelines

When writing about Tap.Giving features:

1. **Lead with the church's problem, not our product**: Don't start with "Tap.Giving offers..." — start with "Your congregation wants to give but..."
2. **Use specific numbers**: "$450 for 100 plates" is more compelling than "affordable pricing"
3. **Include the comparison**: Show what competitors cost alongside our pricing
4. **Address the committee**: Write content a champion can forward to their finance team or elder board
5. **Emphasize simplicity**: Setup is simple, usage is simple, pricing is simple
6. **Be honest about what we don't do**: We're not a payment processor, we don't have an app, we don't offer recurring giving management
7. **Match audience to segment**: A small church plant has different concerns than a 2,000-seat megachurch
