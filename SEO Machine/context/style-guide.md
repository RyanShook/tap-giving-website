# Tap.Giving Style Guide

This guide defines writing conventions, formatting standards, and editorial guidelines for all Tap.Giving content.

---

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Capitalization

**Headlines & Subheadings**:
- **Decision**: Title Case (Capitalize Major Words)

**Product Names**:
- Tap.Giving: Always with capital T, capital G, period between
- Never: TapGiving, Tap Giving, tap.giving, tapgiving
- NFC: Always all caps (Near-Field Communication)

**Industry Terms**:
- church: lowercase unless starting a sentence
- pastor: lowercase unless it's a title ("Pastor Ryan" but "the pastor said")
- NFC: always caps
- tithe/tithing: lowercase
- QR code: QR in caps, "code" lowercase

### Numbers

**When to Spell Out**:
- Spell out: one through nine
- Use numerals: 10 and above
- Exceptions:
  - Percentages: Always use numerals (5%, 300%)
  - Money: Always use numerals ($4.50, $450, $20,000)
  - Quantities: Always use numerals for plates (100 plates, 400+ plates)
  - Stats: Always use numerals for scannability (3x higher, 42x more effective)

**Large Numbers**:
- Use comma: 1,000+
- Spell out million: $1 million
- Use K for casual: $20K/year (acceptable in blog content)

### Punctuation

**Oxford Comma**:
- **Decision**: Yes (Tithely, Pushpay, and Givelify)

**Em Dashes**:
- Style: — (em dash, no spaces)
- Usage: For emphasis or parenthetical statements
- Example: "Our plates work with any platform—Tithely, Pushpay, Givelify, and more."

**Quotation Marks**:
- Straight quotes in code/HTML
- Curly quotes in published prose when possible

**Ellipses**:
- Use sparingly
- Three dots, no spaces: ...

### Abbreviations & Acronyms

**First Use**:
- Spell out on first use: "Near-Field Communication (NFC)"
- Exceptions (universally known, don't spell out): SEO, URL, QR, CTA, FAQ, ROI

**Common Acronyms in Our Space**:
- NFC: Near-Field Communication
- PCI: Payment Card Industry (as in PCI-compliant)
- ROI: Return on Investment
- CTA: Call to Action

**Latin Abbreviations**:
- **Decision**: Avoid in favor of plain English
- Use "for example" not "e.g."
- Use "that is" not "i.e."
- "etc." is acceptable but use "and more" when possible

## Word Choice & Usage

### Preferred Terms

**Say This** → **Not That**:
- tap-to-give → contactless donations (our term is more specific)
- NFC plates → NFC tags/stickers (plates sounds intentional and premium)
- giving page → donation page (church language)
- giving URL → donation link (consistency with "giving page")
- congregants/attenders → users/customers (church vocabulary)
- church → organization (keep it personal)
- one-time cost → CAPEX (avoid jargon)
- works with → integrates with (we're not a software integration)
- modern offering plate → digital collection plate (more natural)
- no app required → app-free (more specific phrasing)
- free shipping → complimentary shipping (keep it simple)

### Words to Avoid
- "Revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive" (hype words)
- "Seamless," "frictionless" (overused in tech — say what you mean specifically)
- "Leverage," "utilize" (corporate-speak — use "use")
- "Solution" as a standalone noun (say what it is: "NFC plates," "tap-to-give hardware")
- "Stakeholders" (say "church leaders," "your team," "the finance committee")
- "Click here" or "read more" (use descriptive link text)

### Inclusive Language
- Use gender-neutral language ("they" as singular)
- "Church leaders" or "pastors" instead of gendered alternatives
- Be inclusive of denominations — don't assume evangelical Protestant context
- Acknowledge that giving practices vary across traditions

## Formatting Standards

### Text Formatting

**Bold**:
- Use for: Key concepts, pricing, important stats, product names on first mention
- Example: "**Tap.Giving** plates cost **$3.50–$4.50 per plate** with **no monthly fees**."

**Italics**:
- Use for: Emphasis (sparingly), publication names
- Example: "The data is clear — NFC is *dramatically* more effective than QR codes."

**ALL CAPS**:
- Only for standard acronyms: NFC, SEO, URL, FAQ, ROI
- Never for emphasis

### Lists

**Bulleted Lists**:
- Capitalize first word
- Period if complete sentence, no period if fragment
- Parallel structure (all sentences or all fragments)

**Numbered Lists**:
- Use for sequential steps only
- Format: 1. 2. 3.
- Complete sentences with periods

### Links

**Anchor Text**:
- Descriptive and keyword-rich
- 2–5 words typically
- YES: "see our Tithely setup guide"
- NO: "click here"

**External Links**:
- Open in new tab
- Link to authoritative sources for statistics

## Content Structure

### Blog Post Introduction
**Standard Structure** (100–200 words):
1. **Hook** (1–2 sentences): Problem, question, or surprising stat
2. **Context** (1–2 sentences): Why this matters for churches
3. **Promise** (1 sentence): What the reader will learn
4. **Keyword** must appear in first 100 words

### Section Length
- **Minimum**: 100 words per section
- **Maximum**: 400 words per section (break into subsections if longer)
- **Ideal**: 200–300 words per main section

### Blog Post Conclusion
**Standard Structure** (100–150 words):
1. **Recap** (2–3 key takeaways)
2. **Action** (What should the reader do next?)
3. **CTA** (Link to order page, pricing, or contact)

## SEO-Specific Style

### Meta Titles
- 50–60 characters including spaces
- Include primary keyword
- Include "| Tap.Giving" if space allows
- No ending punctuation
- Example: "NFC Giving Explained: Church Tech Guide | Tap.Giving"

### Meta Descriptions
- 150–160 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Include call-to-action or value statement
- End with complete thought
- Example: "Everything your church tech team needs to know about NFC giving. How chips work, phone compatibility, security, and setup. No app required."

### URL Slugs
- Lowercase only
- Hyphens between words
- Include primary keyword
- 3–6 words ideal
- Format: `/blog/primary-keyword-phrase.html`
- Example: `/blog/tithely-tap-to-give-setup.html`

### Alt Text
- Describe what image shows
- Include keyword naturally if relevant
- 125 characters or less
- No "image of" or "picture of"

## Dates & Time

**Date Format**: Month DD, YYYY (April 7, 2026)
**Time**: 12-hour with a.m./p.m.: 3:00 p.m.
**Time Zones**: Specify when relevant: "3:00 p.m. CT"

## Statistics & Data

### Citing Sources
- Always cite statistics with sources
- Format: "According to [Source], [statistic]."
- Link to original source when possible
- Include year of data

### Our Key Stats (Always Cite When Using)
- 300%+ donation increase at point of collection
- 81% participation rate with NFC giving
- 3x higher average donation vs. cash
- 42x more effective than QR codes for engagement
- 60% of churchgoers willing to give digitally
- 41% of total donations are digital where offered
- 57% of Protestant churches report increased digital giving

### Presenting Numbers
- Round large numbers: "1.2 million" not "1,234,567"
- Use % symbol: 15% (not "percent")
- Use $ for money: $500 (not "dollars")
- Include commas: 10,000 not 10000

## Brand-Specific Guidelines

### Product References
- "Tap.Giving" (always with period, always capitalized)
- "Tap.Giving NFC plates" (not "the Tap.Giving system")
- "our plates" or "the plates" in casual context

### Competitor References
- **Decision**: Name competitors directly when relevant
- Be fair and factual
- No disparaging language
- Position Tap.Giving as a complement ("Keep Pushpay, add our plates") not a replacement
- Use "competitors" generically when not comparing specifically

### Pricing in Content
- Always current: $3.50–$4.50 per plate
- Always mention: no monthly fees, no transaction fees from us
- Always include context: "starting at $450 for 100 plates"
- Promo code: WELCOME10 for 10% off first order

## Accessibility

### Screen Reader Friendly
- Descriptive link text
- Image alt text for all images
- Proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3, no skipping)

### Plain Language
- Write at 8th–10th grade level
- Define technical terms on first use (NFC, NTAG215, etc.)
- Short sentences and paragraphs
- Break complex ideas into digestible chunks
