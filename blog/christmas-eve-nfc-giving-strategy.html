The Biggest Opportunity Your Church Gets All Year

Christmas Eve isn’t just another service. For most churches, it’s the single highest-attendance event of the entire year—often 2–3x normal Sunday numbers. Families come back. College students visit home. Neighbors who haven’t been inside a church in a decade walk through your doors.

These visitors are emotionally open, spiritually curious, and surrounded by the generosity of the season. It’s the perfect moment to make a first impression that lasts. But here’s what most churches miss: the giving moment is also a connection moment.

Why Christmas Eve Guests Don’t Give (And How to Fix It)

Most first-time visitors want to give. The candlelight service moved them. The music was beautiful. The message spoke to something they needed to hear. But the moment passes and the offering asks them to do something unfamiliar, awkward, or confusing.

“Download our app”

A guest is not going to install an app for a church they may never visit again. Even if they would, finding the app, creating an account, and entering payment info takes 3–5 minutes. The moment is gone.

“Text GIVE to 55555”

Your regulars barely remember this. A first-time visitor will fumble with their phone, mistype the keyword, and give up before the next carol starts.

“I don’t have cash”

Fewer than 10% of Americans carry cash regularly. Your guests came with their phones. Meet them there.

The NFC Fix: “Tap your phone to give”

That’s it. No instructions. No app. No account. The visitor holds their phone near the plate, their browser opens your giving page, and they complete a donation the same way they buy a coffee. The whole process takes under 15 seconds—faster than finding the right app in the App Store.

The Christmas Eve Tap Plate Strategy

Christmas Eve isn’t just about collecting donations. It’s about creating a frictionless moment of generosity that connects guests to your church long after the candles are blown out. Here’s a proven 3-part strategy.

1

Set Up a Christmas-Specific Giving Page

Don’t send guests to your generic giving page. Create a holiday-themed landing page on your giving platform with a warm message: “Thank you for celebrating Christmas Eve with us! Your gift helps us serve our community all year long.”

Pro tip: Include a “Set up recurring giving” checkbox or link on the page. Many Christmas Eve givers are happy to give monthly—they just need the nudge at the right moment. Most giving platforms (Tithely, Planning Center, Subsplash) make this easy to configure.

2

Program Your Plates for the Occasion

NFC tap plates can be programmed with any URL. For Christmas Eve, point them to your holiday giving page instead of your regular one. After the holidays, reprogram them to your standard giving link—it takes 30 seconds per plate with a free NFC app.

Pro tip: Order extra plates specifically for Christmas Eve. At Tap.Giving, plates start at just $3.50 each—cheaper than the bulletins you’re already printing. Use them at every seat, every aisle, and every exit.

3

Introduce It Naturally

Don’t make a big production. A simple, warm introduction is all you need:

“If you’d like to give tonight, just hold your phone near one of the plates at the end of your row. It’ll open right up—no app needed. We’re so glad you’re here.”

That’s 10 seconds. No awkwardness, no guilt, no confusion. Guests who want to give will know exactly what to do. Those who don’t will feel zero pressure.

Where to Place Your Tap Plates for Maximum Impact

Placement matters. Christmas Eve services are often standing-room-only, with extra seating, overflow rooms, and people coming and going between multiple services. Think beyond the offering moment.

Every Row or Section

Place a plate at the end of each row or on every other chair. When people see others tapping, the social proof does the heavy lifting. No announcement needed.

Exit Points

This is the one most churches miss. As people leave the candlelight service, they’re still in the glow of the moment. A tap plate by the door with a simple sign—“Tap to Give”—catches the people who weren’t ready during the offering.

Welcome Table

First-time guests often stop by the welcome table. A tap plate there lets them give before or after the service without any pressure during worship.

Christmas Displays

If your church has a Christmas tree, nativity scene, or photo-op area, place a tap plate nearby. Families taking photos are already engaging with the space—make giving part of that natural moment.

Hot Cocoa & Cookie Tables

Many churches serve refreshments after the Christmas Eve service. It’s a lingering moment. A tap plate on the refreshment table makes giving feel like part of the hospitality, not an obligation.

Kids’ Check-In Area

Parents picking up their kids from children’s ministry are captive for a minute or two. A plate at check-in is another natural touchpoint—especially if you add a small sign: “Love what your kids experienced tonight? Tap to support our children’s ministry.”

How many plates do you need? For Christmas Eve, plan for 1 plate per 8–10 seats, plus 1 at each exit and gathering area. A 500-person Christmas Eve service? Order 60–75 plates. At $3.50–$4.50 each, that’s an investment of $210–$338 for your single biggest giving opportunity of the year. You’ll use them every Sunday afterward.

Turning Christmas Eve Givers Into January Regulars

This is where NFC giving becomes a retention strategy, not just a donation tool. Every person who taps your plate and completes a gift has now entered your giving platform. You have their name and email. That means you can follow up.

D1

Day 1: Thank-You Email

Send a personal, warm thank-you the day after Christmas. Not a receipt—a real message. “We loved having you with us last night. Your generosity is already making an impact.” Include a link to your church’s New Year’s service or January sermon series.

W1

Week 1: Invitation to Return

Invite them to your first Sunday of the new year. Highlight what’s coming—a new series, community events, small groups starting. Give them a reason to come back that isn’t “please give again.”

W3

Week 3: Recurring Giving Nudge

Three weeks after Christmas, send a gentle message about the impact their gift had. Include an easy link to set up recurring giving. “Your Christmas Eve gift helped provide 40 meals to families in need. Want to keep that going?”
