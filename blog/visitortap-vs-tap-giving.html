Giving-First vs. Engagement-First

Different tools built for different purposes

VisitorTap – Engagement Tool

VisitorTap was built by VisitorReach as a guest follow-up and assimilation tool. Giving is secondary. Their “Taptree” feature opens a Linktree-style menu with multiple options – connect cards, event links, social media – before a visitor ever reaches your giving page.

Extra taps = extra friction

Tap.Giving – Giving Tool

Tap.Giving was built to do one thing: get people to your giving page. One tap opens your giving platform directly. No menus, no intermediary pages, no decision fatigue. The shorter the path from impulse to donation, the more people complete it.

One tap = direct to giving

Why this matters for giving

VisitorTap’s “Direct Link” option can send someone to a single URL, but the product’s primary selling point is the multi-link Taptree menu. If your goal is maximizing donations during the service, you want the tool that was purpose-built for giving – not one where giving is a secondary feature bolted onto a guest engagement platform.

The subscription trap

Cancel VisitorTap’s $59.95/month subscription and you lose access to manage your NFC cards through the VisitorReach dashboard. Your cards still work, but you can’t update destinations or view analytics. With Tap.Giving, you own the hardware outright. There is no subscription to cancel because there is no subscription, period.

The 500-Unit Minimum Problem

Most churches don’t need 500 NFC cards

VisitorTap’s Custom Order Requirements

  • 500 unit minimum for any custom-branded order
  • 8 weeks production time for custom orders
  • Standard designs only available in decks of 100
  • A 100-member church buying 500 cards has 400 sitting in a closet

Tap.Giving’s Approach

  • Low 100-plate minimum – 5x lower than VisitorTap’s 500-unit requirement
  • 3–5 weeks delivery for custom branded plates
  • Every order is custom with your church branding
  • Order 5 plates or 500 – same low per-unit price

Size & Durability: Cards vs. Plates

The form factor matters more than you think

VisitorTap Cards & Stickers

  • Credit-card sized (3.37” × 2.12”) – easy to lose, ignore, or pocket
  • Stickers are 3” vinyl – can peel off fabric chairs over time
  • Cards need to be handed out and collected each service
  • Lose cards every week = ongoing replacement costs

Tap.Giving Plates

  • 4” rigid PVC plates designed to mount permanently on pews
  • QR code backup for phones without NFC
  • Always in place – no handing out or collecting
  • Mount once, use for years – zero ongoing effort

Built for the pew, not the pocket

A credit-card-sized NFC card disappears in a pocket or purse. A 4” plate mounted on the pew back is always visible, always accessible, and always prompting generosity. Your congregation doesn’t need to remember to grab a card – the giving option is right there in front of them every Sunday.
