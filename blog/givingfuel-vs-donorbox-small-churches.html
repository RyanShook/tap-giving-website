For small churches, Donorbox is generally the better choice if you want recurring donation tools and customizable embeddable forms — its free tier is robust with a 1.75% platform fee. Quick Answer: Which Platform Wins for Small Churches?

Short answer: If your small church mainly needs simple recurring giving, an embeddable form on your website, and the lowest possible platform fee, pick Donorbox. If you run regular peer-to-peer fundraisers, mission trips, or campaign-driven appeals and want everything in one dashboard, pick GivingFuel. Both work with the giving platform you already announce on Sunday — and both pair cleanly with NFC tap-to-give plates so guests can give in service without typing a URL.

I'm a pastor — I built Tap.Giving because our own church needed an affordable way to add tap-to-give without ripping out our existing giving software. So I want to be honest: this isn't a "one platform crushes the other" story. Donorbox and GivingFuel are both legitimate options for small churches in 2026, and the right pick depends on what your weekend actually looks like.

Below is what each platform does well, where each one frustrates small churches, and how to decide based on your size and giving rhythm.

GivingFuel Pricing & Features (2026)

As publicly listed on the GivingFuel pricing page in 2026 — verify before committing.

What GivingFuel Is

GivingFuel positions itself as an all-in-one online fundraising platform. It's not exclusively for churches — nonprofits, schools, and small ministries use it too — but it has a strong feature set for any organization that runs campaigns alongside ongoing giving. Think: a Christmas appeal, a mission trip fundraiser, a building campaign, plus regular tithes.

Pricing (As Listed in 2026)

  • Free starter tier: donation pages and basic campaign tools, no monthly fee.
  • Transaction fees: approximately 2.9% + $0.30 per credit/debit transaction. ACH is typically lower.
  • Paid tiers: unlock advanced features (text-to-give, custom branding, integrations).
  • Pricing changes: always confirm current rates on the official GivingFuel pricing page.

Strengths for Small Churches

  • Peer-to-peer fundraising built in (great for mission trips, walkathons).
  • Campaign pages with goal thermometers and team fundraising.
  • Friendly setup — designed for non-technical staff/volunteers.
  • One dashboard for recurring giving and special campaigns.

Trade-offs

  • Standard ~2.9% + $0.30 card fee on the free tier — not the lowest in the market.
  • Smaller third-party integration ecosystem than the largest church platforms.
  • If you only need a basic embedded donate form, the campaign features can feel like extra weight.

Donorbox Pricing & Features (2026)

Pricing as publicly listed on the Donorbox pricing page in 2026 — verify before committing.

What Donorbox Is

Donorbox is one of the most widely-used donation platforms for nonprofits and churches under ~300 attendees. It's known for embeddable donation forms, recurring giving tools, and a low platform fee on the free tier. It pairs with Stripe or PayPal as the payment processor, so the church handles processing fees through those rails.

Pricing (As Listed in 2026)

  • Free tier: 1.75% platform fee on top of your payment processor's fees (Stripe is typically ~2.2-2.9% + $0.30).
  • Paid tiers: roughly $40-$150/month for advanced features and a lower platform fee.
  • No setup fees on the free plan; cancel anytime.
  • Always verify current rates on the official Donorbox pricing page.

Strengths for Small Churches

  • Strong recurring donation tools — set-and-forget for tithers.
  • Highly customizable embeddable forms — drop into any church website.
  • Lowest platform fee on a major free tier (1.75% as listed in 2026).
  • Simple donor management and tax-receipt automation.

Trade-offs

  • Less campaign/peer-to-peer functionality than GivingFuel out of the box.
  • Stacked fee structure (platform fee + Stripe fee) can confuse first-time treasurers.
  • To remove or lower the 1.75% platform fee, you'll need a paid tier.

Which Is Better for Small Churches?

Honest answer broken out by your weekend attendance.

Under 50 attendees

Pick: Donorbox. You need the lowest fees and the simplest path to a working donate button. Donorbox's 1.75% platform fee on the free tier is hard to beat at this size, and you probably don't need P2P campaigns yet.

Pair with: a few NFC plates on the welcome desk and front pew.

50-150 attendees

Either works — depends on rhythm. Steady tithing, light campaign activity? Donorbox. Frequent fundraisers (mission trips, retreats, building fund)? GivingFuel's campaign tools earn their keep.

Pair with: 100-pack of NFC plates on every pew or chair.

150-300 attendees

Lean GivingFuel if you're running multiple campaigns a year. Lean toward a Donorbox paid tier if recurring tithing is the dominant flow and you want a cleaner fee on growing transaction volume.

Pair with: 200-pack — every seat covered, lower per-plate cost.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Both platforms are upfront about their headline fees. Here's what surprises church treasurers later — most of which apply to any platform you pick. We wrote a deeper post on this in hidden costs of church giving platforms.

Stacked fees on Donorbox

The 1.75% platform fee is on top of Stripe's processing (~2.2-2.9% + $0.30). On a $100 gift, that's roughly $4-5 total. Don't quote the 1.75% in isolation when reporting to your board.

Add-on features on GivingFuel

Some advanced features (text-to-give, custom domains, deeper integrations) move you off the free tier. Build your wishlist before signing up so you know which tier you actually need.

Donor "cover-the-fee" toggles

Both platforms let donors cover the processing fee. Great for the church bottom line — but verify the default state matches what you actually want presented to first-time givers.

QR code printing & replacement

Most churches end up paying for printed QR signage that gets bent, stained, or lost. NFC plates last for years and don't need to be reprinted when your URL changes — they can be re-encoded.

Is GivingFuel or Donorbox better for small churches?
