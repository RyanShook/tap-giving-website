What Your Church Linktree Should Include

Not every link deserves a spot on your Linktree. You want to keep it focused—just the actions that matter most when someone is sitting in your sanctuary on a Sunday morning. Here are the 7 links we recommend, in order of priority:

Give Now

Link to your Tithely, Subsplash, or Planning Center giving page

This is the primary action. Put it first and make it stand out. When someone taps the plate wanting to give, this should be the first thing they see. Most of your taps will go here.

Connect Card

Google Form, Typeform, or church management system link

First-time visitors can share their name, email, and phone number without fumbling with a paper card and a tiny pencil. Digital connect cards also mean no deciphering handwriting on Monday morning.

Prayer Request

Google Form or your church’s prayer request page

Let people submit prayer requests right from their seat—during the service, during worship, whenever the Spirit moves them. No waiting for the prayer card to come around. No awkwardness of raising a hand in a crowd.

Watch the Sermon

YouTube channel, church website, or podcast link

People who missed last week—or who want to share today’s message with a friend—can find it instantly. This is also great for visitors who want to “check out” your church before committing to a Sunday morning.

Upcoming Events

Church calendar or event registration page

Promote your next big event right where people are already engaged. VBS sign-ups, men’s breakfast, women’s retreat, community service day—whatever’s coming up, put the registration link here.

Join a Small Group

Sign-up form or small group info page

Turn a Sunday visitor into a connected member. This single link can shorten the journey from “I visited once” to “I belong here” by making it effortless to take the next step.

Contact Us

Email, phone, or contact form

Simple but important. Sometimes people just need to reach the church office. A direct link to your contact page or a mailto: link keeps it easy.

How to Set It Up (10 Minutes, Start to Finish)

This is not an exaggeration. You can go from zero to a fully functional Linktree landing page for your NFC plates in about ten minutes. Here’s the step-by-step:

1

Create a Free Linktree Account

Go to linktr.ee and sign up. Use your church email. Pick a clean username like linktr.ee/gracechurch or linktr.ee/yourchurchname. The free plan is all you need.

2

Add Your Links

Click “Add Link” for each item: your giving page URL, connect card form, prayer request form, sermon page, events page, small group sign-up, and contact info. Give each link a clear, action-oriented title.

3

Customize the Look

Use your church’s brand colors for the background and buttons. Upload your church logo as the profile image. Add a short bio like “Welcome! Tap any link below.” Even on the free tier, you have enough customization options to make it look professional.

4

Copy Your Linktree URL

Your Linktree URL will be something like linktr.ee/yourchurch. Copy it. This is the URL you’ll give to Tap.Giving.

5

Order Your Tap.Giving Plates

Head to tap.giving and place your order. When it asks for your giving URL, paste your Linktree URL instead. That’s the only “trick”—your plates can point to any URL, and Linktree is just a URL.

That’s It. You’re Done.

When someone taps the plate, their phone opens your Linktree. They see all your links. They tap the one they want. No app, no download, no login. Just tap and choose.

Pro Tips: Getting the Most Out of Your Linktree

Your Linktree is live and your plates are programmed. Here’s how to squeeze every drop of value out of this setup.

Most Important Link First

Put “Give Now” at the top. It’s the highest-value action and the one most people are looking for after tapping the plate. Everything else follows in descending priority.

Use Action-Oriented Text

Write “Give Now” not “Online Giving Portal.” Write “Submit a Prayer Request” not “Prayer Ministry.” Clear, direct button text gets more clicks than formal labels.

Match Your Church Brand

Use your church’s brand colors for the Linktree background and buttons. Add your logo as the profile image. A branded Linktree feels intentional and professional, not thrown together.

Update Links Seasonally

Swap out event links as seasons change. Add VBS registration in summer. Add Christmas Eve service times in December. Add Easter outreach info in spring. Your Linktree is a living page—keep it current.

Use the Free Analytics

Even on the free tier, Linktree shows you which links get the most clicks. If you see that “Watch the Sermon” gets way more traffic than “Join a Small Group,” that tells you something about what your congregation values. Use the data.

Create Multiple Linktrees

Consider a second Linktree for special events or specific contexts. Point event-specific plates to a different Linktree with event-only links. Your youth room plates could have a different Linktree than your sanctuary plates.

Don’t Forget the Welcome Message

Add a brief description under your church name on Linktree. Something like “Welcome! Tap any link below.” or “Thanks for visiting Grace Church!” This tiny bit of warmth makes the experience feel personal, not transactional.
