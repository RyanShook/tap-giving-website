Subsplash Product Bundles Explained: App, Website, Giving, ChMS

What you're actually paying for inside Subsplash pricing

Custom Mobile App

Branded iOS, Android, and TV apps for sermons, live stream, notifications, and in-app giving. This is the historical core of Subsplash and the driver of most Subsplash app pricing questions.

Church Website

Template-based church website tied to the same Subsplash backend as the app and giving. Subsplash website pricing is usually sold as a standalone plan or inside a bundle.

Subsplash Giving

Online, text, and in-app giving with recurring donations, pledges, and donor records. Covered in more depth in the "What is Subsplash Giving?" section.

ChMS (People / Groups)

Church management: people records, groups, check-in, forms, and communication. This module is typically what pushes Subsplash cost into the $399-$499+/month range.

What is Subsplash Giving?

Subsplash Giving is Subsplash's online and mobile giving product. It powers recurring giving, pledges, text-to-give, and fund designations, and it feeds donation data into the broader Subsplash platform. It is software and payments — it does not include in-person hardware like NFC plates. Churches that want in-pew tap-to-give typically pair Subsplash Giving with a hardware layer like Tap.Giving plates.

Subsplash Giving can be purchased on its own or as part of a bundle. Its pricing is part of overall Subsplash fees, which we break down next.

Hidden Costs of Subsplash

Line items that often don't show up in the headline price

Onboarding & Setup

Initial configuration, training sessions, and content migration may be included or charged separately. Churches should ask whether onboarding is quoted as a one-time fee or wrapped into the first month.

Staff Training Time

A broader Subsplash bundle means more features to learn. That training time is a real cost for small staffs — especially if you're replacing multiple tools at once.

Integrations & Add-ons

Messaging, streaming, advanced ChMS modules, or integrations with third-party tools can add to monthly Subsplash cost. Confirm what is in base price vs. add-on.

Contract Length & Cancellation

Subsplash contracts are often annual. Ask directly about cancellation timing, data export, and whether early termination has a cost before you sign.

Processing Fees on Every Gift

Card processing (~2.9% + $0.30) adds up across every donation. A church taking $500k/year through cards pays roughly $14,500/year in processing alone — separate from the platform fee.

In-Person Giving Hardware

Subsplash Giving is digital/in-app. If you want physical tap-to-give in the room, that hardware is a separate purchase. See our Subsplash Tap vs Tap.Giving comparison.

Frequently Asked Questions About Subsplash Pricing

Straight answers to the most common Subsplash cost questions

How much does Subsplash cost per month?

Subsplash pricing is primarily quote-based. As of 2026, publicly referenced plans typically range from around $69/month for entry-level giving or basic website tools up to $499/month or more for bundled app, website, giving, and ChMS packages. Final Subsplash cost depends on church size, modules selected, and contract length, so churches should request a current quote directly from Subsplash.

What is Subsplash Giving and how does it work?

Subsplash Giving is Subsplash's built-in online giving product. Donors can give through the church's Subsplash mobile app, website, or text-to-give. It handles recurring gifts, pledges, fund designations, and donor records, and it ties giving data into the broader Subsplash platform (ChMS, app, website). Subsplash Giving is a payment and donor management product, not a hardware NFC product.

Does Subsplash charge transaction fees?

Yes. In addition to platform subscription fees, Subsplash Giving passes through standard card and ACH processing fees. Published industry norms for church giving platforms are typically around 2.9% + $0.30 per credit/debit card transaction and roughly 1% for ACH, with exact rates confirmed at signup. Some churches also offer donors the option to cover fees so more of the gift reaches the ministry.

Is Subsplash worth the cost for small churches?

Subsplash can be worth it for churches that genuinely need an all-in-one app, website, giving, and ChMS under one roof. For a small church that mainly needs a simple way to accept digital donations in the room, a full Subsplash bundle can be more software than they will use. In that case, keeping an existing giving platform and adding a low-cost tap-to-give option (like Tap.Giving NFC plates) is often a better fit.

What's the cheapest Subsplash plan?

The cheapest Subsplash plans are typically single-product options, such as a standalone giving plan or a basic website plan. Publicly referenced entry points have been around $69/month, though Subsplash may offer promotional or nonprofit pricing. Churches focused purely on low-cost giving tech should compare this to fee-only platforms or hardware options like Tap.Giving, which has no monthly fee.

Is there a free alternative to Subsplash?

There is no true free, full replacement for Subsplash's bundled feature set. However, churches can replicate much of it by combining free or low-cost tools: a simple website platform, a low-fee giving processor (some charge only transaction fees with no monthly), and optional hardware like Tap.Giving NFC plates for in-person tap-to-give.
