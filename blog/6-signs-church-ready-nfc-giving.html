6 Signs Your Church Is Ready for NFC Tap-to-Give

NFC giving isn’t for every church at every stage. If 3 or more of these signs describe your congregation, you’re ready. If none do, wait six months and check again.

April 12, 2026
6 min read

Quick Scorecard

Read through the 6 signs below and count how many describe your church. 3+ signs = you’re ready. 1–2 signs = you’ll likely benefit but expect slower adoption. 0 signs = focus on building your digital giving foundation first.

1

You Already Have an Online Giving Platform

If your church already uses Tithely, Pushpay, Givelify, Planning Center, Donorbox, or any web-based giving page, you have the infrastructure NFC needs. The plates simply open your existing giving page faster. No new software, no new vendor for payment processing.

Why this matters: NFC isn’t a giving platform—it’s a delivery mechanism. If you don’t have an online giving page yet, set that up first (Tithely’s free tier takes 15 minutes), then add NFC.

2

You Notice Fewer People Carrying Cash

The offering plate comes back lighter each year. It’s not that people are less generous—it’s that 73% of Americans under 40 rarely carry cash. If your ushers are seeing more empty hands during the offering, your congregation is ready for a cashless giving option that works right from the pew.

Why this matters: Those empty hands aren’t reluctant givers. They’re people without a way to give in the moment. NFC gives them one.

3

Your App Adoption Is Low

You launched a church app. Maybe 30% of your congregation downloaded it. Maybe 15% actually use the giving feature. Sound familiar? App fatigue is real—people don’t want another app on their phone. NFC bypasses the app entirely: tap, browser opens, give. No download, no login, no account creation.

Why this matters: NFC reaches the 70% of your church that never downloaded your app and never will. See our guide on church giving without an app.

4

You Get Regular First-Time Visitors

If your church regularly sees 5+ visitors per Sunday, NFC has an outsized impact. 53% of people who give via NFC are giving to that church for the first time. Visitors won’t download your app. They won’t look up your giving URL. But they will tap a plate that’s sitting right in front of them.

Why this matters: A first-time gift is the single strongest predictor of whether a visitor becomes a regular giver. The full visitor→giver strategy →

5

You’re Tired of Monthly Software Fees

If your church is paying $50–$200+/month for giving hardware subscriptions, kiosk fees, or NFC services from other providers, you’re ready for Tap.Giving’s model: one payment, no monthly fees, forever. The money you save in 3–6 months of cancelled subscriptions funds the entire NFC rollout.

Why this matters: A church paying $150/month for a kiosk service spends $1,800/year. Tap.Giving costs $450 once and covers every seat. More on giving cost mistakes →

6

Your Pastor Is Willing to Introduce It from the Stage

This is the most important sign. NFC plates sitting silently on pews get 5% adoption. NFC plates introduced with a confident 90-second demo get 40–60% adoption. If your pastor (or whoever leads the offering) is willing to say, “Watch this” and demo a tap, you’re ready.

Why this matters: The tech is simple. The introduction is what makes it work. We’ve written copy-paste scripts for your first Sunday.

Bonus: 1 Sign You Should Wait

You don’t have any online giving set up yet

NFC plates open a URL. If that URL doesn’t exist yet, the plates are useless. Before ordering NFC, set up a basic online giving page. Tithely’s free tier takes 15 minutes. Givelify is also free. Once you have a giving URL, you’re ready for NFC.
