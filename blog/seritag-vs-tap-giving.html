What DIY Actually Looks Like

Here is everything you would need to do yourself with Seritag tags

1

Choose the right NFC chip

Seritag offers NTAG213, NTAG215, NTAG424, ICODE SLIX, and ICODE SLIX 2. Do you know which one works best for giving? Most churches do not. Pick wrong and your tags may not work reliably with all phones.

2

Design your artwork

You need print-ready artwork for single-sided, full-color printing. That means hiring a designer or spending hours in design software yourself. Most churches do not have in-house designers.

3

Encode every tag

Each NFC tag needs to be individually encoded with the correct URL. You can either do this yourself with an NFC encoding app (one tag at a time) or pay Seritag extra for professional encoding.

4

Set up QR codes

Want a QR code backup for older phones? You will need to generate QR codes, integrate them into your design, and ensure they point to the right destination. Seritag can print them, but you handle the setup.

5

Build your landing page

The NFC tap and QR scan need to go somewhere. You need to set up a giving landing page or configure your existing platform. Seritag provides no help with this.

6

Wait 4+ weeks for production

Seritag's production time is approximately 4 weeks, plus international shipping from the UK. Factor in customs clearance and you could be waiting 5-6 weeks before you even start testing.

7

Test everything

Test every tag with multiple phone models. Verify QR codes scan correctly. Confirm the giving page loads and works. If something is wrong, you troubleshoot it yourself or start over.
