SecureGive Fees Breakdown: Platform, Processing, and Hardware

When churches ask "how much does SecureGive cost?", they're usually surprised by how many separate line items end up on the invoice. SecureGive giving fees generally fall into four buckets:

Monthly platform subscription

Reported at roughly $99-$199/month depending on church size, features, and contract length. This is the recurring SaaS fee for access to the SecureGive giving platform, reporting dashboard, and donor management tools. It renews monthly or annually and is charged even in months with low giving.

Processing / transaction fees

SecureGive passes through card-network and ACH processing fees on every donation. User-reported rates start around 2% for ACH and climb higher for credit-card gifts. Because this is a percentage of every dollar given, a church receiving $300,000/year in electronic gifts could easily pay $6,000-$10,000+ annually in processing alone.

Kiosk hardware & SecureGive Tap devices

SecureGive is well known for physical giving kiosks. Reported prices run $2,000-$5,000+ per kiosk. SecureGive Tap devices (the NFC component) are sold separately and typically require the SecureGive platform underneath. This is upfront capital that lives on church property and requires maintenance.

Setup, training, and add-ons

One-time setup and onboarding is often around $300-$500. Add-on modules (text-to-give, advanced reporting, integrations with ChMS tools) may be extra. Annual contract renewal terms and auto-renewal clauses are common in church giving SaaS and should be reviewed before signing.

Hidden Costs of SecureGive You Might Miss

The monthly fee and transaction fee are just the start. Churches evaluating SecureGive often overlook these line items:

Kiosk maintenance & replacement

Physical kiosks are computers. Screens crack, touch panels wear, software needs updates. Budget for repairs and eventual replacement every 4-6 years.

Integrations & add-ons

Connecting SecureGive to your Church Management System (ChMS) or accounting tool may require paid integrations or third-party connectors.

Staff training

New giving software means training the giving team, finance volunteers, and anyone on stage who announces the giving moment. That's real staff hours you're paying for.

Contract & cancellation terms

Church giving SaaS contracts often run 12 months with auto-renewal. Ask about early-termination fees and whether kiosk hardware is owned outright or tied to the contract.

Switching costs

If you move off SecureGive later, donor data, recurring-gift setups, and integrations all have to be migrated. Building on a platform-agnostic hardware layer avoids this lock-in.

Rate changes over time

Monthly fees and transaction fees aren't locked forever. Card-network price increases typically pass through to churches. Read the fee-change clause in your agreement.
